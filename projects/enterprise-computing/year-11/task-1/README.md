# Guru Driving School — Website Prototype

**Enterprise Computing — Year 11 Preliminary, Assessment Task 1 (2025)**
*"Interactive Media and the User Experience"*

A high-fidelity, interactive website prototype for a fictional driving
school, "Guru Driving School," built entirely in Adobe XD — wireframed,
designed and click-tested as a working prototype rather than shipped as
real HTML/CSS.

## Brief

Promote Guru Driving School (Glenfield) to new L-plate drivers and their
parents/guardians: let visitors compare lesson price tiers, see what each
tier includes, and book with an instructor — while staying clean,
accessible and trustworthy enough that a nervous first-time customer
would follow through on a booking.

## Process

- **Project scope definition** — functional/aesthetic criteria table, each
  row justified against *why it matters*, *how it'd be tested*, and *the
  impact of missing it* (e.g. a broken booking system → lost customers).
- **Analysis** — accessibility (colour contrast, readable type, large nav
  targets) and data-privacy considerations under the Privacy Act 1988:
  only necessary customer data collected, with consent, never sold on.
- **Wireframing → requirements analysis** — functional requirements (nav
  bar, contact form, consistent footer) paired with non-functional detail
  (visual polish, confirmation messaging, consistency across pages).
- **Gantt chart + logbook** — a 10-week dated build log (research →
  sketches → wireframing in Adobe XD → nav bar → home page → remaining
  pages → testing), including real problems hit along the way (images
  pasting in sideways, Adobe XD's learning curve).
- **Testing and evaluating** — a feature-by-feature criteria/result/
  improvement table for the nav bar, footer, media, error page and
  booking flow.

## What worked, what didn't

Nav bar, footer, error page, map and video all worked as intended and
held up under testing. Two of the booking options broke through to an
error page rather than the payment window. Some imagery/video was lower
quality than intended, reading as less professional than planned.
Because it's an Adobe XD prototype rather than real code, the site has no
real interactivity — forms can't actually be typed into or submitted.

**Reflection:** the honest limitation of prototyping in Adobe XD instead
of hand-coding is exactly that — no real HTML/CSS meant no working forms,
no real data storage, no actual animation. Next iteration: build it for
real.
